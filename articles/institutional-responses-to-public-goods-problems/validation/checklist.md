# Public Goods Validation Checklist

Before adapting this scaffold to a real case, review:

- [ ] Is the public good clearly defined?
- [ ] Is the good pure, mixed, club-like, or common-pool?
- [ ] Are contribution rules documented?
- [ ] Are free-riding opportunities documented?
- [ ] Are trust and legitimacy assessed?
- [ ] Is monitoring assessed for fairness?
- [ ] Is enforcement assessed for legitimacy?
- [ ] Are marginalized communities visible in the analysis?
- [ ] Are benefits and burdens separately assessed?
- [ ] Is capture risk assessed?
- [ ] Are qualitative sources included?
- [ ] Are historical sources included?
- [ ] Are assumptions documented?
- [ ] Is uncertainty documented?
- [ ] Are results framed as interpretive rather than deterministic?
- [ ] Is the analysis free from workplace surveillance or individual assessment uses?
