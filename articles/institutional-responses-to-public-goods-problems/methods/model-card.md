# Model Card: Public Goods Institution Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore public goods contribution, provision quality, free-riding, trust, legitimacy, enforcement, monitoring, capture risk, and distributional burden.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive compliance teams
- automated decision systems
- surveillance systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing contribution and governance conditions.

## Outputs

- contribution rate
- provision quality
- high provision indicator
- fragile public good indicator
- high burden risk indicator
- dynamic contribution simulation results
- capture risk sensitivity
- distributional burden review

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no substitute for historical or qualitative research

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- treating distrust as irrational
- labeling exclusion as free-riding
- ignoring marginalized communities
- legitimizing coercive provision
- using public goods models to justify preselected policy agendas
