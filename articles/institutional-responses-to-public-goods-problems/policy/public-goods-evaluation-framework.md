# Public Goods Evaluation Framework

## Provision quality criteria

A public goods institution should be evaluated across multiple criteria:

1. **Contribution stability**  
   Are contributions durable over time?

2. **Provision quality**  
   Is the public good reliably produced and maintained?

3. **Legitimacy**  
   Are contribution rules perceived as fair, justified, and accountable?

4. **Monitoring fairness**  
   Is monitoring transparent, proportionate, and equitable?

5. **Enforcement fit**  
   Does enforcement deter free-riding without undermining trust?

6. **Distributional justice**  
   Are burdens and benefits distributed fairly?

7. **Scale fit**  
   Does the governance level match the problem level?

8. **Capture resistance**  
   Are mechanisms in place to prevent narrow-interest capture?

9. **Learning capacity**  
   Can the institution detect failure and adapt?

## Outcome typology

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| Under-provision | Contributions are insufficient for the public good |
| Coerced provision | Provision exists but depends heavily on threat |
| Fragile provision | Provision exists but legitimacy is weak |
| Captured provision | Public good rhetoric masks narrow benefits |
| Burden-shifting provision | Benefits are broad but burdens are unequal |
| Legitimate provision | Contribution, provision, legitimacy, and fairness align |
