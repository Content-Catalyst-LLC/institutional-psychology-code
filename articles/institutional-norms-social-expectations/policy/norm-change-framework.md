# Norm Change Framework

## Norm-change criteria

A norm-change system should be evaluated across multiple criteria:

1. **Visibility**  
   Are alternative behaviors visible enough to reduce uncertainty?

2. **Safety**  
   Can people adopt new conduct without excessive sanction, retaliation, or exclusion?

3. **Legitimacy**  
   Is the alternative norm perceived as fair, useful, and institutionally meaningful?

4. **Reinforcement**  
   Are desired behaviors rewarded, protected, repeated, and recognized?

5. **Learning**  
   Can dissent, refusal, and failure inform redesign?

6. **Burden reduction**  
   Does the new norm reduce unequal normative burden?

7. **Accountability**  
   Does the norm change include mechanisms for correction?

8. **Memory**  
   Does the institution remember why the old norm needed change?

## Outcome typology

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| Legitimate coordination | Norms support cooperation, fairness, trust, and accountability |
| Surface conformity | People follow expectations because they must, not because they accept them |
| Fragile normative stability | Apparent stability exists while expectation convergence and legitimacy are weak |
| Suppressive norm environment | Social enforcement punishes dissent or whistleblowing |
| Performative norm change | Language changes while informal reinforcement remains intact |
| Justice-centered norm change | Affected people help define new expectations and institutional incentives change |
