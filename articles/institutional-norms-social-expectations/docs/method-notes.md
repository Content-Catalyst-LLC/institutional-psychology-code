# Method Notes

## Why model norms and expectations?

Institutions depend on behavior becoming predictable enough for coordination. Norms and expectations reduce uncertainty, support compliance, lower monitoring costs, transmit role expectations, and stabilize institutional life.

A useful model can separate:

- norm repetition
- expectation convergence
- internalization
- social enforcement
- legitimacy alignment
- trust reinforcement
- role clarity
- learning capacity
- alternative norm visibility
- sanction cost
- suppressive pressure
- fragmentation pressure
- unequal normative burden
- rigidity pressure

## Why synthetic data?

Synthetic data allows transparent demonstration without using confidential records, vulnerable communities, employees, students, patients, regulated entities, citizens, or sensitive administrative information.

## Why include justice and historical memory?

Norms can stabilize cooperation, but they can also normalize exclusion, silence, hierarchy, and unequal recognition. What appears normal to one group may feel coercive to another.

## What this scaffold cannot do

It cannot score real normality. It cannot classify real people as deviant or conforming. It cannot justify discipline, surveillance, or culture policing. It cannot replace legal analysis, public participation, historical context, qualitative research, or domain expertise.
