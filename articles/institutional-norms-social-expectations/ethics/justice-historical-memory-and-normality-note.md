# Justice, Historical Memory, and the Burden of Normality

Norms are not neutral simply because they are familiar.

A justice-sensitive norm analysis should ask:

- Who defines what counts as normal?
- Who must do extra work to appear professional, credible, civil, or compliant?
- Who is allowed to deviate without penalty?
- Whose anger, urgency, or refusal is punished?
- Whose knowledge is treated as evidence?
- Who benefits when the norm is treated as natural?
- Can affected people contest the norm itself, or only request exceptions within it?
- Is norm-change language being used to avoid material repair?

Norms should not replace enforceable rights, due process, appeal, transparency, or accountability.
