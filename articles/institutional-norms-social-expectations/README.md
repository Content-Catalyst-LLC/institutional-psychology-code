# Institutional Norms and Social Expectations

This advanced companion repository supports the article **Institutional Norms and Social Expectations** in the Institutional Psychology knowledge series.

The repository provides a research-grade, synthetic-data scaffold for studying how norm repetition, expectation convergence, internalization, social enforcement, legitimacy alignment, trust reinforcement, role clarity, learning capacity, alternative norm visibility, sanction cost, suppressive pressure, fragmentation pressure, unequal normative burden, and rigidity pressure shape normative stability, compliance, coordination, norm change, and institutional learning.

## Research purpose

This repository is designed for:

- synthetic-data research
- institutional norm modeling
- social expectation simulation
- normative-stability analysis
- expectation-convergence diagnostics
- compliance and coordination modeling
- social-enforcement review
- suppressive norm-environment assessment
- norm-change readiness analysis
- legitimacy-alignment modeling
- unequal normative burden review
- public governance, courts, workplaces, schools, healthcare, platform governance, environmental governance, professional ethics, regulatory systems, and civic institutions examples
- reproducible multi-language workflows
- teaching and public-interest methods demonstration

It is not designed for automated decision-making about real people, employees, citizens, patients, students, applicants, agencies, firms, communities, or institutions.

## Core research question

When do institutional norms and social expectations generate legitimate coordination, trust, cooperation, and behavioral stability — and when do they produce brittle conformity, suppressive silence, unequal burden, performative compliance, exclusion, or resistance to necessary institutional change?

## Main variables

| Variable | Meaning |
|---|---|
| norm_repetition | Visible repetition and routine enactment of an institutional norm |
| expectation_convergence | Agreement about what others do and what others expect |
| internalization | Degree to which actors follow the norm without direct monitoring |
| social_enforcement | Approval, disapproval, inclusion, exclusion, and reputation pressure |
| legitimacy_alignment | Perceived fairness, rightfulness, and appropriateness of the norm |
| trust_reinforcement | Expectation that others will follow the norm consistently and in good faith |
| role_clarity | Clarity of expected conduct within institutional roles |
| learning_capacity | Ability to revise norms based on feedback, dissent, failure, and repair |
| alternative_norm_visibility | Visibility of new conduct that could become a competing norm |
| sanction_cost | Cost of deviation from the existing norm |
| suppressive_pressure | Degree to which enforcement suppresses dissent, whistleblowing, challenge, or learning |
| fragmentation_pressure | Conflict, rival norms, subgroup divergence, or unstable expectation pressure |
| unequal_normative_burden | Extra work required from some groups to appear normal, credible, professional, or compliant |
| rigidity_pressure | Resistance to norm change embedded in routines, incentives, identity, or hierarchy |
| normative_stability | Composite synthetic norm-stability score |
| high_coordination | Binary threshold indicator for high coordination |
| fragile_normative_environment | High apparent stability with weak expectation convergence and weak legitimacy alignment |
| suppressive_norm_environment | Strong social enforcement plus high suppressive pressure and weak learning capacity |
| norm_change_readiness | Composite synthetic score for readiness to shift norms |

## Responsible-use note

All data is synthetic. The repository is intended for conceptual modeling, public-interest research, education, and transparent methods demonstration.

Do not use this repository for:

- employee screening
- employment selection
- hiring
- promotion
- compensation
- discipline
- termination
- workplace surveillance
- individual performance management
- psychological assessment
- predictive policing
- automated eligibility determination
- coercive public administration
- norm scoring of real people, groups, communities, or institutions
- conformity scoring of real people, groups, communities, or institutions
- legitimacy scoring of real people, groups, communities, or institutions
- ranking real communities, agencies, firms, workers, students, or institutions without context
- punishing dissent, refusal, or whistleblowing
- treating deviation as irrational or pathological when it may reveal institutional harm
- using culture or norms language to suppress critique
- replacing rights, appeal, accountability, and repair with appeals to social expectations
- extracting testimony from affected communities without changing decisions
- legitimacy laundering for already-decided reforms, dashboards, culture programs, risk frameworks, compliance reviews, or disciplinary regimes

Any real-world adaptation must include legal review, ethical review, qualitative evidence, historical analysis, stakeholder participation, burden audits, affected-community voice, privacy review, and attention to marginalized communities.

## Suggested workflow

1. Read `docs/research-design.md`.
2. Review `ethics/responsible-use-boundaries.md`.
3. Generate synthetic norms data with `python/generate_institutional_norms_data.py`.
4. Run dynamic norm simulation with `python/simulate_norm_dynamics.py`.
5. Run fragile normative environment diagnostics with `python/fragile_normative_environment_review.py`.
6. Run suppressive norm environment review with `python/suppressive_norm_environment_review.py`.
7. Run norm change sensitivity analysis with `python/norm_change_sensitivity.py`.
8. Analyze norm stability and coordination in R with `r/institutional_norms_modeling.R`.
9. Create SQLite schema with `sql/schema.sql`.
10. Review `validation/validation-plan.md` and `validation/checklist.md`.
11. Use `docs/github-embed-wordpress.html` for the WordPress GitHub block.
