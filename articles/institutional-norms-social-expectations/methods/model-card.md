# Model Card: Institutional Norms and Social Expectations Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore normative stability, expectation convergence, internalization, social enforcement, legitimacy alignment, trust reinforcement, role clarity, learning capacity, suppressive pressure, fragmentation pressure, unequal normative burden, and norm-change readiness.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers
- organizational researchers
- norm and social-expectation researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive culture programs
- automated monitoring systems
- surveillance systems
- norm scoring systems
- conformity scoring systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing norm and expectation conditions.

## Outputs

- normative stability score
- high-coordination indicator
- fragile normative-environment indicator
- suppressive norm-environment indicator
- norm-change readiness score
- norm and expectation review tables

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no classification of real actors or communities
- no substitute for legal, historical, qualitative, decision-audit, or institutional expertise

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- norm scoring real communities
- treating dissent as deviance
- punishing whistleblowing
- replacing rights with conformity
- ignoring affected-community knowledge
- legitimizing symbolic culture change
- using norm models to manage criticism rather than repair institutions
