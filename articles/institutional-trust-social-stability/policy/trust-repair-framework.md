# Trust Repair Framework

## Trust-repair criteria

A trust-repair system should be evaluated across multiple criteria:

1. **Acknowledgment**  
   Does the institution clearly name the failure without euphemism or blame shifting?

2. **Explanation**  
   Does the institution explain what happened, why it happened, and who was affected?

3. **Accountability**  
   Are responsibility and consequences credible?

4. **Correction**  
   Are procedures, incentives, authority structures, data systems, or decisions changed?

5. **Restitution**  
   Are affected people materially helped where possible?

6. **Recognition**  
   Are affected communities treated as legitimate knowers and participants?

7. **Memory**  
   Is harm preserved in institutional memory so recurrence is less likely?

8. **Verification**  
   Can affected people and external reviewers observe whether repair is real?

## Outcome typology

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| Earned trust | Institutional conduct makes confidence reasonable |
| Surface compliance | People comply because they must, not because they trust |
| Fragile trust | Apparent trust exists while fairness and accountability are weak |
| High-distrust pressure | Arbitrariness and visible violation pressure are high while repair is weak |
| Symbolic repair | Messaging or apology appears without material change |
| Justice-centered repair | Affected people help define repair and institutional design changes |
