# Justice, Historical Memory, and Trust Note

Trust is not owed in advance. It is earned through institutional conduct.

A justice-sensitive trust analysis should ask:

- Who is asked to trust?
- What has the institution done to earn trust from historically harmed groups?
- Whose distrust is treated as irrational?
- Who bears the burden of proving institutional harm?
- Does the institution preserve memory of harm?
- Does repair change procedures, incentives, authority, and access?
- Are affected communities asked to participate without influence?
- Is trust-building language being used to avoid accountability?

Trust should not replace enforceable rights, due process, appeal, transparency, or material repair.
