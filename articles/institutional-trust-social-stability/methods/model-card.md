# Model Card: Institutional Trust and Social Stability Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore trust, legitimacy, fairness, accountability, recognition, repair capacity, voluntary compliance, social stability, administrative burden, arbitrariness pressure, visible violations, and fragmentation pressure.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers
- organizational researchers
- trust and legitimacy researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive culture programs
- automated monitoring systems
- surveillance systems
- trust scoring systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing institutional trust conditions.

## Outputs

- institutional trust score
- social stability score
- high-trust indicator
- high-stability indicator
- fragile trust-environment indicator
- high-distrust pressure indicator
- trust-repair sensitivity analysis
- institutional trust review tables

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no classification of real actors or communities
- no substitute for legal, historical, qualitative, decision-audit, or institutional expertise

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- trust scoring real communities
- treating distrust as irrational
- replacing rights with trust appeals
- ignoring affected-community knowledge
- legitimizing symbolic trust repair
- using trust models to manage criticism rather than repair institutions
