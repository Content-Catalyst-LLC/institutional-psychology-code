# Method Notes

## Why model institutional trust?

Institutional trust enables cooperation under uncertainty. In complex societies, people cannot verify every agency, court, platform, database, school, hospital, regulator, or administrative process. Trust lowers verification costs and supports voluntary coordination.

A useful model can separate:

- consistency
- competence
- fairness
- transparency
- accountability
- integrity
- recognition and voice
- repair capacity
- legitimacy
- voluntary compliance
- cooperation capacity
- learning repair
- arbitrariness pressure
- visible violation pressure
- fragmentation pressure
- administrative burden

## Why synthetic data?

Synthetic data allows transparent demonstration without using confidential records, vulnerable communities, employees, students, patients, regulated entities, citizens, or sensitive administrative information.

## Why include justice and historical memory?

Trust is not distributed evenly. Institutions meet people through histories of protection, exclusion, surveillance, neglect, displacement, violence, dignity, repair, and burden. Distrust can be rational and evidence-based.

## What this scaffold cannot do

It cannot score real trustworthiness. It cannot classify communities as trusting or distrustful. It cannot justify coercion, surveillance, or messaging campaigns. It cannot replace legal analysis, public participation, historical context, qualitative research, or domain expertise.
