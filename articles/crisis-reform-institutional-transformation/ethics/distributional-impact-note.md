# Distributional Impact Note

Crisis is rarely evenly distributed. Institutional breakdown often harms those with the least protection, least voice, and least capacity to absorb disruption.

A crisis/reform analysis should ask:

- Who was already exposed to institutional failure before the crisis became visible?
- Who experienced the greatest harm?
- Who absorbed institutional workload?
- Whose knowledge was ignored?
- Who was invited into reform design?
- Who gained authority after the crisis?
- Who was blamed?
- Who was protected?
- Did reform reduce vulnerability or shift it elsewhere?

A reform can look successful at the aggregate level while leaving the deepest harms untouched.
