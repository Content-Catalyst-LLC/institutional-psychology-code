# Method Notes

## Why model crisis and reform?

Institutional crisis can compress time, expose hidden failure, weaken path dependence, and open reform windows. However, crisis does not automatically produce meaningful transformation. Models help separate:

- crisis pressure
- reform possibility
- reform capacity
- reform legitimacy
- reform capture risk
- transformation depth

## Why synthetic data?

Synthetic data allows transparent demonstration without using confidential records, vulnerable communities, real employees, or politically sensitive institutional data.

## Why include capture risk?

A reform window can be captured by powerful actors. Reform may preserve the appearance of correction while protecting existing authority, redistributing blame, or excluding affected communities.

## Why include distributional attention?

Institutional crisis often harms marginalized groups earlier and more severely. A reform model that ignores unequal burdens may mistake stabilization for justice.

## What this scaffold cannot do

It cannot determine whether a real institution should be reformed, replaced, or preserved. It cannot identify moral responsibility. It cannot substitute for community voice, legal analysis, archival evidence, public inquiry, or historical research.
