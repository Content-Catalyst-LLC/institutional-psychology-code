# Model Card: Institutional Change and Behavioral Adaptation Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore institutional change, behavioral adaptation, transition burden, fragile adaptation, and recursive feedback over time.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive compliance teams
- automated decision systems
- surveillance systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing institutional change and adaptation conditions.

## Outputs

- institutional change score
- successful adaptation indicator
- fragile adaptation indicator
- transition burden diagnostics
- recursive adaptation simulation results
- distributional attention review

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no substitute for historical or qualitative research

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- ignoring marginalized communities
- treating adaptation as automatically positive
- legitimizing burden shifting
- using adaptation models to justify preselected reforms
