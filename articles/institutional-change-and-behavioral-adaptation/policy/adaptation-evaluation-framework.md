# Institutional Adaptation Evaluation Framework

## Adaptation quality criteria

An institutional adaptation should be evaluated across multiple criteria:

1. **Behavioral enactment**  
   Did actors actually change practice?

2. **Legitimacy**  
   Is the change perceived as fair, intelligible, and justified?

3. **Governance capacity**  
   Can the institution implement, monitor, and revise the change?

4. **Feedback responsiveness**  
   Does the institution detect and act on signals?

5. **Coordination quality**  
   Are units, actors, and levels aligned?

6. **Transition support**  
   Are affected actors supported during change?

7. **Distributional justice**  
   Are burdens and benefits distributed fairly?

8. **Durability**  
   Can the adaptation persist without coercive overreach or trust erosion?

## Adaptation outcome typology

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| Symbolic change | Language changes but behavior does not |
| Behavioral lag | Rules change faster than routines |
| Fragile adaptation | Change occurs but legitimacy is weak |
| Burden-shifting adaptation | Continuity is preserved by moving costs elsewhere |
| Coherent adaptation | Rules, behavior, legitimacy, and governance align |
| Transformational adaptation | Institutional logic, behavior, and authority meaningfully shift |
