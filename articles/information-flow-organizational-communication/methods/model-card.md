# Model Card: Information Flow and Organizational Communication Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore information-flow effectiveness, signal quality, communication quality, interpretive integration, feedback usability, memory retention, openness, escalation access, trust, community voice, digital transparency, distortion loss, overload, siloing, suppression pressure, and metric tunnel vision.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers
- organizational researchers
- communication-system researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive communication systems
- automated monitoring systems
- surveillance systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing communication-system conditions.

## Outputs

- information effectiveness score
- high integration indicator
- fragile communication indicator
- high overload system indicator
- dynamic information-flow simulation results
- distortion sensitivity analysis
- fragile communication review

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no classification of real actors or communities
- no substitute for legal, historical, qualitative, communication-audit, or institutional expertise

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- treating dashboards as reality
- treating silence as agreement
- ignoring affected-community knowledge
- legitimizing symbolic feedback systems
- using communication models to manage dissent rather than revise decisions
