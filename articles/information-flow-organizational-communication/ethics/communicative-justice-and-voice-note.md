# Communicative Justice and Voice Note

Institutional communication is not neutral.

A justice-sensitive communication analysis should ask:

- Whose knowledge becomes official?
- Whose warnings are dismissed?
- Who must translate experience into institutional language before being believed?
- Who is repeatedly asked to provide evidence without receiving decision power?
- Who bears the cost when information does not travel?
- Can affected communities contest official summaries?
- Does feedback create revision, or merely legitimacy?
- Does communication reduce harm, or improve institutional self-protection?

Communication becomes accountable only when voice can change memory, decisions, routines, incentives, policy, or governance.
