# Model Card: Compliance and Rule-Following Behavior Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore compliance quality, legitimacy, fairness, incentive alignment, norm support, enforcement credibility, communication quality, cognitive clarity, trust, adaptive learning, compliance burden, selective rule application, defensive compliance, hypocrisy visibility, and norm failure.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers
- regulatory policy researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive compliance teams
- automated decision systems
- surveillance systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing compliance and rule-following conditions.

## Outputs

- compliance quality score
- high compliance indicator
- fragile compliance indicator
- high burden compliance indicator
- dynamic rule-following simulation results
- selective rule-application sensitivity analysis
- compliance burden review

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no classification of real actors or communities
- no substitute for legal, historical, qualitative, or institutional expertise

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- treating ambiguity or incapacity as noncompliance
- ignoring affected communities
- legitimizing coercive rule systems
- using compliance models to discipline visible actors while ignoring power
