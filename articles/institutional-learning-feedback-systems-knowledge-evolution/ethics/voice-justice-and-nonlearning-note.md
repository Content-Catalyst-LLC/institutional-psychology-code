# Voice, Justice, and Institutional Non-Learning Note

Institutions do not learn equally from everyone.

A justice-sensitive learning analysis should ask:

- Whose experience is treated as evidence?
- Whose evidence is dismissed as anecdotal?
- Who must suffer repeatedly before assumptions change?
- Who bears the cost of institutional non-learning?
- Who participates in defining what counts as improvement?
- Who is repeatedly asked to teach the institution?
- Does feedback create revision, or only legitimacy?
- Does learning reduce harm, or improve self-protection?

Institutional non-learning can become a form of harm when institutions repeatedly receive evidence and fail to revise assumptions, routines, incentives, or governance.
