# Model Card: Institutional Incentives and Behavioral Responses Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore incentive effectiveness, value alignment, fairness, information quality, legitimacy, learning support, accountability, cognitive bias pressure, metric substitution, reporting distortion, behavioral burden, short-termism, status inequality, and motivation crowding.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers
- organizational researchers
- behavioral policy researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive performance systems
- automated decision systems
- surveillance systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing incentive and behavioral-response conditions.

## Outputs

- incentive effectiveness score
- high alignment indicator
- fragile incentive-system indicator
- high burden incentive-system indicator
- dynamic incentive simulation results
- metric-substitution sensitivity analysis
- incentive-burden review

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no classification of real actors or communities
- no substitute for legal, historical, qualitative, or institutional expertise

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- treating metric performance as real value
- ignoring affected communities
- legitimizing coercive incentive systems
- using incentive models to discipline visible actors while ignoring power
