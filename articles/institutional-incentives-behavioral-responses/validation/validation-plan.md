# Validation Plan

A real incentive and behavioral-response study should validate claims across several layers.

## 1. Construct validity

Confirm that indicators actually measure intended concepts.

Examples:

- Does "incentive effectiveness" measure value alignment rather than target achievement?
- Does "information quality" capture truthfulness and completeness rather than report volume?
- Does "metric substitution" measure proxy replacement of institutional purpose?
- Does "burden" include workload, documentation, coordination, moral, and psychological costs?

## 2. Behavioral validation

Compare incentive design with actual behavior:

- aligned effort
- metric optimization
- gaming
- defensive reporting
- error disclosure
- mission drift
- short-termism
- turnover or disengagement
- motivation crowding
- risk displacement

## 3. Institutional validation

Assess whether formal incentives, metrics, rewards, penalties, information flows, learning systems, and accountability systems are documented.

## 4. Process-tracing validation

Ask whether the proposed sequence is plausible:

```text
incentive design → interpretation → behavioral response → information effect → performance signal → learning or distortion
```

## 5. Sensitivity analysis

Test whether conclusions change when:

- legitimacy is lower
- fairness is lower
- information quality is lower
- metric substitution is higher
- reporting distortion is higher
- behavioral burden is higher
- accountability is lower
- learning support is included or excluded

## 6. Distributional validation

Check whether aggregate performance hides unequal reward, burden, risk, visibility, or hidden labor.

## 7. Participatory validation

Invite affected communities, frontline staff, low-resource actors, monitored actors, domain experts, public-interest advocates, and marginalized groups to review interpretation.
