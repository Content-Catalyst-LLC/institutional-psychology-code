# Regulatory Behavior and Institutional Accountability

This advanced companion repository supports the article **Regulatory Behavior and Institutional Accountability** in the Institutional Psychology knowledge series.

The repository provides a research-grade, synthetic-data scaffold for studying how rules, oversight systems, enforcement structures, reporting regimes, institutional learning, legitimacy, information quality, capture pressure, accountability reach, regulatory burden, and unequal accountability shape behavior in complex institutional systems.

## Research purpose

This repository is designed for:

- synthetic-data research
- regulatory accountability modeling
- oversight and information-quality analysis
- enforcement-legitimacy interaction analysis
- capture-pressure diagnostics
- regulatory burden review
- accountability-reach analysis
- fragile regulation diagnostics
- high-burden regulatory-system review
- evasion and performative compliance analysis
- public administration and regulatory behavior examples
- financial, environmental, platform, and organizational governance examples
- reproducible multi-language workflows
- teaching and public-interest methods demonstration

It is not designed for automated decision-making about real people, employees, citizens, patients, students, applicants, agencies, firms, communities, or institutions.

## Core research question

When does regulation become behaviorally real accountability rather than symbolic oversight, performative compliance, unequal burden, capture, or documentation without correction?

## Conceptual frame

The scaffold models regulatory accountability effectiveness as a function of:

- oversight strength
- legitimacy
- incentive alignment
- enforcement credibility
- information quality
- adaptive learning
- accountability reach
- capture pressure
- regulatory burden
- evasion pressure
- hypocrisy visibility
- unequal accountability
- reporting quality
- contestability
- affected-community voice

## Main variables

| Variable | Meaning |
|---|---|
| oversight_strength | Capacity, independence, authority, and intensity of oversight |
| legitimacy | Perceived fairness, rightfulness, and public/institutional credibility of regulation |
| incentive_alignment | Degree to which incentives support regulatory purpose rather than gaming |
| enforcement_credibility | Consistency, proportionality, and likelihood of meaningful enforcement |
| information_quality | Accuracy, completeness, auditability, and usability of regulatory information |
| adaptive_learning | Capacity to revise rules, standards, and practice from feedback |
| accountability_reach | Degree to which accountability reaches powerful actors and not only visible actors |
| capture_pressure | Pressure from regulated interests, political influence, information dependence, or proximity |
| regulatory_burden | Cost, complexity, documentation, time, and psychological burden of compliance |
| evasion_pressure | Incentive or opportunity to avoid, game, delay, or conceal compliance |
| hypocrisy_visibility | Visibility of powerful actors avoiding accountability |
| unequal_accountability | Uneven monitoring, sanction, reporting burden, or consequence across actors |
| accountability_effectiveness | Composite synthetic regulatory-accountability score |
| high_accountability | Binary threshold indicator for strong regulatory accountability |
| fragile_regulation | High apparent accountability with low legitimacy |
| high_burden_regulation | High apparent accountability with high burden and unequal accountability |

## Responsible-use note

All data is synthetic. The repository is intended for conceptual modeling, public-interest research, education, and transparent methods demonstration.

Do not use this repository for:

- employee screening
- employment selection
- hiring
- promotion
- compensation
- discipline
- termination
- workplace surveillance
- individual performance management
- psychological assessment
- predictive policing
- automated eligibility determination
- coercive public administration
- ranking real communities, agencies, firms, or institutions without context
- suppressing dissent under the language of compliance
- labeling inability, exclusion, confusion, or distrust as evasion
- legitimacy laundering for already-decided regulatory or governance agendas

Any real-world adaptation must include legal review, ethical review, qualitative evidence, historical analysis, stakeholder participation, burden audits, affected-community voice, and attention to marginalized communities.

## Suggested workflow

1. Read `docs/research-design.md`.
2. Review `ethics/responsible-use-boundaries.md`.
3. Generate synthetic regulatory accountability data with `python/generate_regulatory_accountability_data.py`.
4. Run dynamic regulatory simulation with `python/simulate_regulatory_behavior.py`.
5. Run fragile regulation diagnostics with `python/fragile_regulation_review.py`.
6. Run regulatory burden review with `python/regulatory_burden_review.py`.
7. Run capture pressure sensitivity analysis with `python/capture_pressure_sensitivity.py`.
8. Analyze regulatory accountability outcomes in R with `r/regulatory_accountability_modeling.R`.
9. Create SQLite schema with `sql/schema.sql`.
10. Review `validation/validation-plan.md` and `validation/checklist.md`.
11. Use `docs/github-embed-wordpress.html` for the WordPress GitHub block.
