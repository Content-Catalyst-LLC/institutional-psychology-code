# Method Notes

## Why model regulatory behavior?

Regulation is a behavioral institution. It shapes what actors see, fear, document, hide, correct, report, contest, and learn from. It can produce substantive accountability, but it can also generate defensive documentation, strategic compliance, capture, evasion, and unequal burden.

A useful model can separate:

- oversight strength
- legitimacy
- incentive alignment
- enforcement credibility
- information quality
- adaptive learning
- accountability reach
- capture pressure
- regulatory burden
- evasion pressure
- hypocrisy visibility
- unequal accountability

## Why synthetic data?

Synthetic data allows transparent demonstration without using confidential records, vulnerable communities, employees, students, patients, regulated entities, citizens, or sensitive administrative information.

## Why include fragile regulation?

A regulatory system can appear strong because it produces reports, audits, findings, and sanctions while still lacking legitimacy, independence, information quality, or accountability reach.

## Why include burden and unequal accountability?

Regulatory burden and accountability reach determine whether a system disciplines power or merely disciplines the already visible. Aggregate compliance may hide unequal cost, scrutiny, sanction, or protection.

## What this scaffold cannot do

It cannot classify real firms, agencies, or institutions as captured or compliant. It cannot substitute for legal analysis, community participation, historical context, qualitative research, or regulatory expertise.
