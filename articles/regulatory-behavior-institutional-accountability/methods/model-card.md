# Model Card: Regulatory Behavior and Institutional Accountability Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore regulatory accountability, oversight strength, legitimacy, incentive alignment, enforcement credibility, information quality, adaptive learning, accountability reach, capture pressure, regulatory burden, evasion pressure, hypocrisy visibility, and unequal accountability.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers
- regulatory policy researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive compliance teams
- automated decision systems
- surveillance systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing regulatory and accountability conditions.

## Outputs

- accountability effectiveness score
- high accountability indicator
- fragile regulation indicator
- high burden regulation indicator
- dynamic regulatory simulation results
- capture pressure sensitivity analysis
- regulatory burden review

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no classification of real actors or communities
- no substitute for legal, historical, qualitative, or regulatory expertise

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- treating ambiguity or incapacity as evasion
- ignoring affected communities
- legitimizing coercive regulation
- using accountability models to discipline visible actors while ignoring power
