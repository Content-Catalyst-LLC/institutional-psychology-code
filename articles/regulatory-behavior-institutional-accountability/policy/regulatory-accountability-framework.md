# Regulatory Accountability Framework

## Accountability quality criteria

A regulatory accountability system should be evaluated across multiple criteria:

1. **Rule clarity**  
   Are obligations, standards, and reporting duties understandable?

2. **Oversight independence**  
   Can oversight act without improper influence?

3. **Information quality**  
   Can the system see relevant behavior and risk?

4. **Enforcement credibility**  
   Are consequences consistent, proportional, and meaningful?

5. **Legitimacy**  
   Are rules and enforcement perceived as fair, justified, and public-purpose aligned?

6. **Accountability reach**  
   Does accountability reach powerful actors?

7. **Burden justice**  
   Are compliance costs and reporting burdens necessary, proportionate, and fairly distributed?

8. **Capture resistance**  
   Are regulated interests prevented from dominating rule interpretation?

9. **Learning capacity**  
   Do findings lead to correction, rule revision, and institutional memory?

10. **Contestability**  
   Can affected actors challenge rules, classifications, and enforcement decisions?

## Outcome typology

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| Formal regulation | Rules exist but behavioral effect is uncertain |
| Performative accountability | Reports and audits exist without correction |
| Captured regulation | Oversight aligns with regulated interests |
| High-burden regulation | Accountability exists but costs are unequally distributed |
| Fragile regulation | Apparent accountability exists with weak legitimacy |
| Legitimate accountability | Oversight, information, enforcement, learning, fairness, and accountability reach align |
