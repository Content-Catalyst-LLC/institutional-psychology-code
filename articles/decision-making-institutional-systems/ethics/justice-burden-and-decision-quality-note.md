# Justice, Burden, and Decision Quality Note

Decision quality is not only about efficiency, coherence, or procedural order. It also depends on whether the decision system can recognize unequal burden, marginalized knowledge, affected-community evidence, and contestable assumptions.

A justice-sensitive decision analysis should ask:

- Who helped define the problem?
- Who was consulted only after the frame was fixed?
- Whose evidence was treated as credible?
- Whose evidence was treated as anecdotal?
- Who bears the cost if the institution is wrong?
- What harms are visible in the metrics?
- What harms remain outside the official decision record?
- Can affected actors challenge interpretation, not only outcome?

Institutions should not use decision-quality language to avoid democratic contestation or affected-community accountability.
