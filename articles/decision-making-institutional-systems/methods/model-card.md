# Model Card: Decision-Making in Institutional Systems Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore decision quality, bounded-rationality pressure, organizational structure, incentive alignment, information flow, legitimacy, uncertainty management, corrective capacity, justice-sensitive voice, memory quality, feedback openness, bias distortion, power protection, metric fixation, siloing, and premature closure.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers
- organizational researchers
- decision-quality researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive culture programs
- automated monitoring systems
- surveillance systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing institutional decision-system conditions.

## Outputs

- decision quality score
- high-quality decision indicator
- fragile decision-environment indicator
- high-distortion environment indicator
- dynamic decision simulation results
- decision architecture sensitivity analysis
- decision-quality review tables

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no classification of real actors or communities
- no substitute for legal, historical, qualitative, decision-audit, or institutional expertise

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- replacing public participation with scoring
- treating dominant assumptions as objective
- ignoring affected-community knowledge
- legitimizing symbolic review
- using decision-quality models to manage criticism rather than revise decisions
