# Validation Plan

A real institutional decision-quality study should validate claims across several layers.

## 1. Construct validity

Confirm that indicators actually measure intended concepts.

Examples:

- Does "decision quality" measure reasoning quality rather than outcome favorability?
- Does "legitimacy" measure meaningful procedural fairness rather than formal participation?
- Does "justice voice" measure influence on the decision frame, not just consultation?
- Does "corrective capacity" actually alter assumptions and decisions?
- Does "information-flow effectiveness" preserve context, not only speed?

## 2. Behavioral validation

Compare formal decision systems with actual decision behavior:

- assumption tracking
- evidence inclusion and exclusion
- decision rights
- challenge and dissent patterns
- forecast calibration
- post-decision review
- treatment of negative evidence
- dashboard use and metric substitution
- community evidence uptake
- decision traceability

## 3. Institutional validation

Assess whether decision templates, evidence standards, dashboards, review processes, escalation rules, audit paths, and governance forums are documented.

## 4. Process-tracing validation

Ask whether the proposed sequence is plausible:

```text
problem definition → evidence selection → interpretation → decision → implementation → outcome → feedback → memory → revision
```

## 5. Sensitivity analysis

Test whether conclusions change when:

- information flow is lower
- corrective capacity is lower
- legitimacy is lower
- uncertainty management is lower
- justice voice is lower
- feedback openness is lower
- bounded-rationality pressure is higher
- bias distortion is higher
- power protection is higher
- metric fixation is higher

## 6. Distributional validation

Check whether aggregate decision metrics hide unequal voice, burden, exclusion, complaint patterns, or affected-community knowledge.

## 7. Participatory validation

Invite affected communities, frontline staff, low-resource actors, monitored actors, domain experts, public-interest advocates, and marginalized groups to review interpretation.
