# Institutional Repair and Learning Framework

## Repair and learning criteria

An institutional repair and learning system should be evaluated across multiple criteria:

1. **Acknowledgment**  
   Does the institution name failure clearly and accurately?

2. **Memory**  
   Does the institution preserve the history of harm or only the official response?

3. **Explanation**  
   Does the institution explain what happened, why it happened, and who was affected?

4. **Accountability**  
   Are responsibility and consequences visible?

5. **Correction**  
   Are procedures, incentives, authority relationships, data systems, or decision rules changed?

6. **Burden reduction**  
   Does the institution reduce proof, waiting, administrative friction, or unequal burden?

7. **Participation**  
   Are affected people involved in defining repair?

8. **Verification**  
   Can repair be independently reviewed?

9. **Learning continuity**  
   Does the institution preserve lessons across staff turnover, leadership change, and future crises?

## Outcome typology

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| Behavioral institutional reality | Formal rules are recognized, enacted, trusted, and remembered |
| Surface compliance | People perform institutional conduct without substantive trust or legitimacy |
| Fragile institutional environment | Apparent strength exists while legitimacy and norms are weak |
| High-fragmentation environment | Rival expectations, opacity, and weak repair threaten institutional alignment |
| Symbolic repair | Messaging or review occurs without changed behavior |
| Justice-centered repair | Affected people help define repair and institutional behavior changes |
