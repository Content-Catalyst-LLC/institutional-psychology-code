# Justice, Memory, and Institutional Behavior Note

Institutional stability is not the same as justice.

A justice-sensitive institutional analysis should ask:

- Who is asked to comply?
- Whose trust is presumed rather than earned?
- Whose distrust is historically grounded?
- Who carries administrative burden?
- Who can contest institutional interpretation?
- Whose institutional memory is preserved?
- Does repair change authority, procedure, data systems, incentives, and burden?
- Is behavioral analysis being used to repair institutions or manipulate compliance?

Institutions should not replace enforceable rights, due process, appeal, transparency, accountability, or material repair with behavioral framing.
