# Responsible-Use Boundaries

This repository is for synthetic-data research, institutional learning, teaching, public-interest analysis, and transparent methods demonstration.

## Do not use this repository for

- employee screening
- employment selection
- hiring
- firing
- promotion
- compensation
- discipline
- termination
- workplace surveillance
- individual performance management
- psychological assessment
- predictive policing
- automated eligibility determination
- ranking real people, firms, agencies, workers, students, or communities
- coercive public administration
- institutional scoring real communities, workers, students, patients, applicants, or institutions
- legitimacy scoring real communities, workers, students, patients, applicants, or institutions
- behavior scoring real communities, workers, students, patients, applicants, or institutions
- trust scoring real communities, workers, students, patients, applicants, or institutions
- treating distrust as irrational when it may be historically informed
- using institutional psychology to manipulate compliance
- replacing enforceable rights, due process, appeal, or accountability with behavioral nudging
- extracting testimony from affected communities without changing decisions
- legitimacy laundering for already-decided reforms, dashboards, reviews, risk frameworks, compliance systems, or disciplinary regimes

## Ethical requirements for real-world adaptation

Any real-world adaptation should include:

- legal review
- ethics review
- privacy review
- domain expertise
- participatory governance
- affected-community consultation
- burden audits
- historical analysis
- qualitative evidence
- distributional impact analysis
- documentation of uncertainty
- transparency about assumptions
- human accountability
- appeal and contestation mechanisms

## Institutional psychology caution

Institutional-behavior models can be misused when institutions treat distrust, refusal, dissent, or noncompliance as a psychology problem rather than as evidence of institutional burden, historical harm, unequal recognition, opacity, arbitrary treatment, or uncorrected failure. Models should support institutional repair and accountability, not discipline critics.
