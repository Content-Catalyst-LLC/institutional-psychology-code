# Institutions and Human Behavior

This advanced companion repository supports the article **Institutions and Human Behavior** in the Institutional Psychology knowledge series.

The repository provides a research-grade, synthetic-data scaffold for studying how normative stability, legitimacy strength, incentive alignment, information quality, memory retention, learning capacity, trust reinforcement, role clarity, repair capacity, administrative burden, opacity pressure, historical harm pressure, and fragmentation pressure shape institutional strength, behavioral alignment, fragility, and institutional learning.

## Research purpose

This repository is designed for:

- synthetic-data research
- institutional-behavior modeling
- behavioral-alignment simulation
- institutional durability analysis
- legitimacy and trust modeling
- normative-stability diagnostics
- information-quality review
- institutional-memory modeling
- learning-capacity simulation
- fragmentation-pressure assessment
- fragile institutional-environment review
- high-fragmentation environment analysis
- repair-capacity modeling
- administrative-burden review
- historical-harm and institutional-memory analysis
- public governance, courts, organizations, schools, healthcare, regulatory systems, digital platforms, environmental governance, and sustainability systems examples
- reproducible multi-language workflows
- teaching and public-interest methods demonstration

It is not designed for automated decision-making about real people, employees, citizens, patients, students, applicants, agencies, firms, communities, or institutions.

## Core research question

How do institutions become behaviorally real through legitimacy, norms, trust, information, memory, incentives, role performance, and learning — and when do these behavioral foundations weaken through fragmentation, opacity, burden, historical harm, distrust, and failed repair?

## Conceptual frame

The scaffold models institutional behavior as a recursive system in which institutions shape cognition, norms, roles, incentives, information, and trust, while human behavior reproduces, revises, contests, or destabilizes the institutional system over time.

Key concepts include:

- normative stability
- legitimacy strength
- incentive alignment
- information quality
- memory retention
- learning capacity
- trust reinforcement
- role clarity
- repair capacity
- administrative burden
- opacity pressure
- historical harm pressure
- fragmentation pressure
- institutional strength
- behavioral alignment
- fragile institutional environments
- high-fragmentation environments
- institutional repair and learning

## Main variables

| Variable | Meaning |
|---|---|
| normative_stability | Stability of shared expectations, role conduct, informal rules, and norm systems |
| legitimacy_strength | Perceived rightfulness, fairness, and acceptability of institutional authority |
| incentive_alignment | Alignment between incentives and institutionally desired behavior |
| information_quality | Clarity, accessibility, accuracy, timeliness, and interpretability of institutional information |
| memory_retention | Capacity to preserve precedent, records, institutional memory, and learning context |
| learning_capacity | Capacity to absorb feedback, correct failure, and revise institutional behavior |
| trust_reinforcement | Expectation that the institution and other actors will act consistently and in good faith |
| role_clarity | Clarity of institutional roles, obligations, responsibilities, and expected conduct |
| repair_capacity | Capacity to acknowledge, correct, and repair institutional failure |
| administrative_burden | Procedural work, proof burden, delay, and friction imposed by institutions |
| opacity_pressure | Difficulty understanding or contesting institutional decisions |
| historical_harm_pressure | Weight of remembered or documented institutional harm, exclusion, or betrayal |
| fragmentation_pressure | Rival expectations, distrust, subgroup divergence, and narrative conflict |
| institutional_strength | Composite synthetic score for institutional behavioral reality and durability |
| behavioral_alignment | Composite synthetic score for alignment between behavior and institutional expectations |
| high_institutional_alignment | Binary threshold indicator for strong institutional alignment |
| high_behavioral_alignment | Binary threshold indicator for strong behavioral alignment |
| fragile_institutional_environment | High apparent strength with weak legitimacy and weak normative stability |
| high_fragmentation_environment | Strong fragmentation and opacity with weak repair capacity |

## Responsible-use note

All data is synthetic. The repository is intended for conceptual modeling, public-interest research, education, and transparent methods demonstration.

Do not use this repository for:

- employee screening
- employment selection
- hiring
- promotion
- compensation
- discipline
- termination
- workplace surveillance
- individual performance management
- psychological assessment
- predictive policing
- automated eligibility determination
- coercive public administration
- institutional scoring of real people, groups, communities, or institutions
- legitimacy scoring of real people, groups, communities, or institutions
- behavior scoring of real people, groups, communities, or institutions
- trust scoring of real people, groups, communities, or institutions
- ranking real communities, agencies, firms, workers, students, or institutions without context
- suppressing dissent, refusal, or whistleblowing
- treating distrust as irrational when it may be historically informed
- using institutional psychology language to manipulate compliance
- replacing rights, appeal, accountability, and repair with behavioral nudging
- extracting testimony from affected communities without changing decisions
- legitimacy laundering for already-decided reforms, dashboards, reviews, risk frameworks, compliance systems, or disciplinary regimes

Any real-world adaptation must include legal review, ethical review, qualitative evidence, historical analysis, stakeholder participation, burden audits, affected-community voice, privacy review, and attention to marginalized communities.

## Suggested workflow

1. Read `docs/research-design.md`.
2. Review `ethics/responsible-use-boundaries.md`.
3. Generate synthetic institutional-behavior data with `python/generate_institutions_behavior_data.py`.
4. Run dynamic institutional-behavior simulation with `python/simulate_institution_behavior_dynamics.py`.
5. Run fragile institutional environment diagnostics with `python/fragile_institutional_environment_review.py`.
6. Run high-fragmentation environment review with `python/high_fragmentation_environment_review.py`.
7. Run repair and learning sensitivity analysis with `python/institutional_repair_learning_sensitivity.py`.
8. Analyze institutional strength and behavioral alignment in R with `r/institutions_behavior_modeling.R`.
9. Create SQLite schema with `sql/schema.sql`.
10. Review `validation/validation-plan.md` and `validation/checklist.md`.
11. Use `docs/github-embed-wordpress.html` for the WordPress GitHub block.
