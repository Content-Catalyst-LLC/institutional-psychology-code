# Model Card: Institutions and Human Behavior Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore institutional strength, behavioral alignment, legitimacy strength, normative stability, incentive alignment, information quality, memory retention, learning capacity, trust reinforcement, role clarity, repair capacity, administrative burden, opacity pressure, historical harm pressure, and fragmentation pressure.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers
- organizational researchers
- institutional psychology researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive culture programs
- automated monitoring systems
- surveillance systems
- institutional scoring systems
- legitimacy scoring systems
- behavior scoring systems
- trust scoring systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing institutional-behavior conditions.

## Outputs

- institutional strength score
- behavioral alignment score
- high institutional-alignment indicator
- high behavioral-alignment indicator
- fragile institutional-environment indicator
- high-fragmentation environment indicator
- repair and learning sensitivity outputs
- institutional-behavior review tables

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no classification of real actors or communities
- no substitute for legal, historical, qualitative, decision-audit, or institutional expertise

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- scoring real institutions or communities
- treating distrust as irrational
- replacing rights with compliance management
- ignoring affected-community knowledge
- legitimizing symbolic repair
- using institutional psychology to manage criticism rather than repair institutions
