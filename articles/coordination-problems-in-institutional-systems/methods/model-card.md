# Model Card: Institutional Coordination Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore coordination quality, expectation alignment, focal-point salience, authority signals, communication clarity, uncertainty, competing standards, fragile coordination, and high-burden coordination.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive compliance teams
- automated decision systems
- surveillance systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing coordination and governance conditions.

## Outputs

- coordination quality
- high alignment indicator
- fragile coordination indicator
- high burden coordination indicator
- dynamic coordination simulation results
- competing standards sensitivity
- adaptation burden review

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no substitute for historical or qualitative research

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- treating dissent as miscoordination
- ignoring marginalized communities
- legitimizing coercive standardization
- using coordination models to justify preselected authority claims
