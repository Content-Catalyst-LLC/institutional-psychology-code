# Coordination Evaluation Framework

## Coordination quality criteria

An institutional coordination system should be evaluated across multiple criteria:

1. **Shared expectation clarity**  
   Do actors understand what action is expected?

2. **Focal-point salience**  
   Is the coordinating signal or standard mutually recognizable?

3. **Authority legitimacy**  
   Do actors recognize the signaler as rightful and credible?

4. **Communication reliability**  
   Are signals consistent, timely, and accessible?

5. **Interoperability**  
   Can systems, organizations, or procedures work together?

6. **Timing and sequencing**  
   Are handoffs, dependencies, deadlines, and action sequences clear?

7. **Trust support**  
   Do actors believe others will align?

8. **Adaptation burden management**  
   Are switching and compliance costs supported fairly?

9. **Learning capacity**  
   Does the institution detect and correct misalignment?

## Outcome typology

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| Fragmented coordination | Actors split across standards or signals |
| Delayed coordination | Actors align only after costly hesitation |
| Fragile coordination | Alignment exists but trust or legitimacy is weak |
| High-burden coordination | Alignment exists but adaptation costs are unequal |
| Coercive coordination | Alignment is imposed without meaningful participation |
| Adaptive coordination | Signals, standards, feedback, trust, and learning reinforce one another |
