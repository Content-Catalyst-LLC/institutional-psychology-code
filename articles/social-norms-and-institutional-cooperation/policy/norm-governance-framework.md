# Norm Governance Framework

## Norm quality criteria

A social norm should be evaluated across multiple criteria:

1. **Behavioral clarity**  
   Do actors understand what conduct is expected?

2. **Legitimacy**  
   Is the norm perceived as fair, justified, and aligned with institutional purpose?

3. **Reciprocity**  
   Do actors believe others are expected to comply as well?

4. **Fair enforcement**  
   Are sanctions proportionate, transparent, and not selectively applied?

5. **Transmission quality**  
   Is the norm taught through practice, leadership, training, and memory?

6. **Contestability**  
   Can actors challenge the norm without retaliation?

7. **Distributional fairness**  
   Are compliance burdens distributed fairly?

8. **Learning capacity**  
   Can the institution revise norms when they become harmful, obsolete, or unjust?

## Outcome typology

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| Weak norm | Shared expectation is unclear or inconsistently enacted |
| Performative norm | Public endorsement exceeds substantive behavior |
| Fragile norm | Compliance exists but trust or legitimacy is weak |
| Captured norm | Norm protects dominant actors or hierarchy |
| High-burden norm | Norm imposes unequal compliance costs |
| Legitimate norm | Expectation, trust, legitimacy, fairness, and cooperation align |
