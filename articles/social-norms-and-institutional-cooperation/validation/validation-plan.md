# Validation Plan

A real social norms study should validate claims across several layers.

## 1. Construct validity

Confirm that indicators actually measure intended concepts.

Examples:

- Does "descriptive norm" measure what actors believe others do?
- Does "injunctive norm" measure approval/disapproval rather than personal preference?
- Does "cooperation" reflect substantive behavior rather than performative compliance?

## 2. Behavioral validation

Compare norm claims with actual behavior:

- compliance behavior
- informal sanction patterns
- reporting behavior
- observed routines
- meeting interaction
- peer feedback
- reputation dynamics

## 3. Institutional validation

Assess whether formal rules, leadership signals, training, incentives, and informal practices are aligned.

## 4. Process-tracing validation

Ask whether the proposed sequence is plausible:

```text
expectation → social reinforcement → compliance → trust update → norm strength update
```

## 5. Sensitivity analysis

Test whether conclusions change when:

- trust is lower
- legitimacy is lower
- norm conflict is higher
- hypocrisy is more visible
- unequal enforcement increases
- performative compliance is included
- distributional attention is included or excluded

## 6. Distributional validation

Check whether aggregate norm compliance hides unequal burden, social risk, scrutiny, or sanction.

## 7. Participatory validation

Invite affected communities, frontline staff, low-power actors, domain experts, and marginalized groups to review interpretation.
