# Model Card: Social Norms and Institutional Cooperation Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore norm-based cooperation, descriptive norms, injunctive norms, trust, legitimacy, social sanctions, transmission, institutional reinforcement, norm conflict, hypocrisy, unequal enforcement, and performative compliance.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive compliance teams
- automated decision systems
- surveillance systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing norm and cooperation conditions.

## Outputs

- cooperation score
- high norm compliance indicator
- fragile norm environment indicator
- high burden norm environment indicator
- dynamic norm formation and erosion simulation results
- norm conflict sensitivity
- unequal enforcement review

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no substitute for historical or qualitative research

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- treating dissent as deviance
- ignoring marginalized communities
- legitimizing coercive conformity
- using norm models to justify preselected culture-change agendas
