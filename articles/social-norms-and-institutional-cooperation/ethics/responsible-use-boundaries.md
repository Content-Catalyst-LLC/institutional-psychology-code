# Responsible-Use Boundaries

This repository is for synthetic-data research, institutional learning, teaching, public-interest analysis, and transparent methods demonstration.

## Do not use this repository for

- employee screening
- employment selection
- hiring
- firing
- promotion
- compensation
- discipline
- termination
- workplace surveillance
- individual performance management
- psychological assessment
- predictive policing
- automated eligibility determination
- ranking real people or communities
- coercive public administration
- suppressing dissent under the language of norm enforcement
- enforcing conformity without accountability
- replacing participatory culture work
- legitimizing already-decided culture-change agendas

## Ethical requirements for real-world adaptation

Any real-world adaptation should include:

- legal review
- ethics review
- participatory governance
- community consultation
- historical analysis
- qualitative evidence
- distributional impact analysis
- documentation of uncertainty
- transparency about assumptions
- human accountability
- appeal and contestation mechanisms

## Institutional psychology caution

Norm models can be misused when institutions label dissent, whistleblowing, refusal, distrust, or legitimate objection as simple norm violation. Models should support reflection, not replace judgment.
