# Research Design: Institutional Resilience

## Conceptual framing

Institutional resilience is the capacity of institutional systems to absorb disruption, adapt to changing conditions, preserve core functions under stress, and reorganize when necessary without losing legitimacy, normative coherence, or operational credibility.

This companion research scaffold treats institutional resilience as a dynamic interaction among:

- structural capacity
- behavioral cooperation
- trust
- legitimacy
- feedback quality
- learning rate
- redundancy
- modularity
- coordination
- shock exposure

## Analytic strategy

The synthetic-data workflows support four complementary approaches:

1. **Index construction**  
   Build a composite resilience index from theoretically meaningful variables.

2. **Continuity modeling**  
   Estimate whether institutions preserve core functions under stress.

3. **Stress testing**  
   Simulate stronger shocks and observe changes in continuity.

4. **Dynamic simulation**  
   Model repeated exposure over time as trust, legitimacy, and adaptive capacity update.

## Interpretive cautions

Institutional resilience is not automatically good. Some institutions are resilient because they preserve exclusion, coercion, inequality, or unjust risk distribution. Therefore, resilience analysis must ask:

- Resilient for whom?
- At whose expense?
- Through which mechanisms?
- With what effect on legitimacy?
- With what consequences for marginalized communities?
- Does resilience preserve public purpose or merely institutional survival?

## Data caution

All data in this repository is synthetic. It should not be interpreted as real institutional measurement.
