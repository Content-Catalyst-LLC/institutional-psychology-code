# Model Card: Behavioral Foundations of Governance Systems Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore behavioral governance effectiveness, legitimacy, trust, cognitive interpretability, norm support, enforcement credibility, adaptive learning, perceived fairness, behavioral burden, hypocrisy visibility, and power asymmetry.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive compliance teams
- automated decision systems
- surveillance systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing behavioral and institutional governance conditions.

## Outputs

- governance effectiveness score
- high governance indicator
- fragile governance indicator
- high burden governance indicator
- dynamic governance simulation results
- enforcement-legitimacy sensitivity analysis
- burden review

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no classification of real actors or communities
- no substitute for historical or qualitative research

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- treating confusion as noncompliance
- ignoring marginalized communities
- legitimizing coercive governance
- using behavioral design to make burdensome systems more efficient rather than more just
