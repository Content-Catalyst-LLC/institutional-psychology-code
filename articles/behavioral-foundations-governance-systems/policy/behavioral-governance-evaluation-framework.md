# Behavioral Governance Evaluation Framework

## Governance quality criteria

A behavioral governance system should be evaluated across multiple criteria:

1. **Legitimacy**  
   Are authority, procedure, and outcomes perceived as fair and justified?

2. **Cognitive interpretability**  
   Can people understand what the system requires?

3. **Realistic compliance**  
   Can people comply without excessive burden?

4. **Fair enforcement**  
   Is enforcement credible, consistent, proportional, and not selectively applied?

5. **Incentive alignment**  
   Do metrics, rewards, and penalties support actual institutional purpose?

6. **Norm alignment**  
   Do informal expectations support formal rules and accountability?

7. **Trust support**  
   Does the system build or undermine confidence over time?

8. **Adaptive learning**  
   Can the institution detect error and revise practice?

9. **Contestability**  
   Can affected actors challenge rules, classifications, and decisions?

10. **Burden justice**  
   Are behavioral, administrative, and psychological burdens visible and fairly distributed?

## Outcome typology

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| Formal governance | Rules exist but behavioral workability is unclear |
| Fragile governance | Performance exists but trust or legitimacy is weak |
| High-burden governance | Performance exists but compliance costs are unequal |
| Coercive governance | Compliance is secured through fear, force, or lack of alternatives |
| Performative governance | Indicators improve while underlying governance does not |
| Legitimate governance | Rules, trust, fairness, accountability, learning, and burden justice align |
