# Validation Plan

A real behavioral governance study should validate claims across several layers.

## 1. Construct validity

Confirm that indicators actually measure intended concepts.

Examples:

- Does "legitimacy" measure perceived rightfulness rather than fear of sanction?
- Does "cognitive interpretability" measure actual rule comprehension?
- Does "governance effectiveness" capture substantive governance rather than performance theater?
- Does "burden" include learning, compliance, psychological, and appeal costs?

## 2. Behavioral validation

Compare governance design with actual behavior:

- compliance behavior
- errors and delays
- appeal rates
- help requests
- workarounds
- gaming behavior
- participation patterns
- informal enforcement

## 3. Institutional validation

Assess whether formal rules, incentives, enforcement, communication, feedback, and accountability systems are documented.

## 4. Process-tracing validation

Ask whether the proposed sequence is plausible:

```text
governance design → interpretation → trust/legitimacy → compliance/cooperation → feedback → adaptation
```

## 5. Sensitivity analysis

Test whether conclusions change when:

- legitimacy is lower
- trust is lower
- burden is higher
- hypocrisy is visible
- power asymmetry increases
- cognitive interpretability decreases
- enforcement becomes more or less credible
- adaptive learning is included or excluded

## 6. Distributional validation

Check whether aggregate performance hides unequal burden, monitoring, sanction, appeal costs, or exclusion.

## 7. Participatory validation

Invite affected communities, frontline staff, low-resource actors, marginalized groups, domain experts, and public-interest stakeholders to review interpretation.
