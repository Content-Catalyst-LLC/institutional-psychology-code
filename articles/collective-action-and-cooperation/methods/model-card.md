# Model Card: Collective Action and Cooperation Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore collective action capacity, cooperation, trust, legitimacy, norms, enforcement, communication, coordination, fairness, free-riding pressure, burden inequality, hypocrisy, and scale complexity.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive compliance teams
- automated decision systems
- surveillance systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing cooperation and institutional conditions.

## Outputs

- cooperation score
- high cooperation indicator
- fragile cooperation indicator
- high burden cooperation indicator
- dynamic collective action simulation results
- free-riding sensitivity
- burden inequality review

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no classification of real actors as free-riders
- no substitute for historical or qualitative research

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- treating noncooperation as selfishness
- ignoring marginalized communities
- legitimizing coercive cooperation
- using cooperation models to justify preselected policy agendas
