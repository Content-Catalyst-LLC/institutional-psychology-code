# Method Notes

## Why model collective action?

Collective action problems reveal how institutions transform scattered individual capacities into shared provision, public goods, common resource protection, organizational collaboration, and collective risk management.

A useful model can separate:

- incentive alignment
- trust
- legitimacy
- norms
- enforcement
- communication
- coordination
- perceived fairness
- free-riding pressure
- burden inequality
- hypocrisy visibility
- scale complexity

## Why synthetic data?

Synthetic data allows transparent demonstration without using confidential records, vulnerable communities, employees, students, patients, citizens, or sensitive institutional information.

## Why include fragile cooperation?

A collective action system can appear successful while resting on low trust, coercion, hidden resentment, elite hypocrisy, or unequal burden.

## Why include burden inequality?

Aggregate cooperation can hide who pays, who benefits, who is monitored, who is blamed, and who performs hidden coordination labor.

## What this scaffold cannot do

It cannot classify real actors as cooperative or free-riding. It cannot determine the legitimacy of real institutions. It cannot substitute for community voice, legal analysis, historical context, qualitative research, or participatory governance.
