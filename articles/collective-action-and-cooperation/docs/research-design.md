# Research Design: Collective Action and Cooperation

## Conceptual framing

This research scaffold treats collective action as the institutional production of shared capacity under uncertainty. Institutions enable cooperation when they align incentives, build trust, sustain legitimacy, reinforce norms, make contribution visible, reduce free-riding, communicate clearly, and distribute burdens fairly.

The scaffold distinguishes:

1. **Contribution behavior**  
   Whether actors participate, cooperate, contribute, comply, share, or coordinate.

2. **Cooperation quality**  
   Whether contribution is effective, durable, legitimate, and connected to collective outcomes.

3. **Free-riding pressure**  
   The perceived opportunity or incentive to benefit without contributing.

4. **Fragile cooperation**  
   High cooperation paired with low trust, low legitimacy, visible hypocrisy, or coercive dependence.

5. **High-burden cooperation**  
   Cooperation that appears successful in aggregate while imposing disproportionate costs on less powerful or lower-capacity actors.

6. **Collective action repair**  
   Institutional work to restore trust, clarify rules, adjust burdens, strengthen communication, and make contribution meaningful again.

## Core hypotheses

### H1: Trust increases collective action capacity.

Actors are more likely to cooperate when they believe others will also cooperate and institutions will administer contributions fairly.

### H2: Legitimacy strengthens enforcement and voluntary cooperation.

Enforcement is more durable when perceived as procedurally fair, proportional, and institutionally legitimate.

### H3: Norm strength supports cooperation when reciprocity is credible.

Cooperative norms can stabilize contribution, especially when actors believe others are expected to cooperate as well.

### H4: Communication quality reduces uncertainty.

Clear information about risks, contribution, participation, and outcomes improves cooperation by creating shared situational awareness.

### H5: Free-riding pressure undermines cooperation.

Visible or tolerated defection reduces trust, raises resentment, and weakens contribution norms.

### H6: Aggregate cooperation can hide inequality.

High cooperation may still be fragile or unjust if burdens are unequally distributed or hidden contributions are ignored.

## Research design options

- synthetic cross-sectional modeling
- repeated collective action simulation
- trust and legitimacy threshold analysis
- free-riding sensitivity analysis
- enforcement-legitimacy interaction analysis
- norm-trust interaction analysis
- public goods provision review
- common-pool resource case study
- collective risk governance analysis
- burden inequality diagnostics
- networked cooperation models
- qualitative process tracing

## Responsible interpretation

The model does not determine whether any real group, institution, or community is cooperative or noncooperative. It structures reasoning about contribution, trust, legitimacy, free-riding, fairness, and institutional design. Real institutional analysis requires legal context, qualitative inquiry, historical evidence, public participation, and justice-sensitive interpretation.
