# Collective Action Evaluation Framework

## Cooperation quality criteria

A collective action system should be evaluated across multiple criteria:

1. **Contribution clarity**  
   Do actors understand what contribution is expected?

2. **Trust**  
   Do actors believe others will cooperate?

3. **Legitimacy**  
   Are rules, procedures, and authority perceived as fair and justified?

4. **Fair burden sharing**  
   Are contribution costs and risks distributed fairly?

5. **Free-riding control**  
   Is opportunistic noncontribution monitored and addressed proportionately?

6. **Communication quality**  
   Is information clear, credible, timely, and accessible?

7. **Coordination quality**  
   Are timing, roles, standards, and expectations aligned?

8. **Learning capacity**  
   Can the institution detect cooperation breakdown and adapt?

9. **Contestability**  
   Can actors challenge unfair cooperation demands?

## Outcome typology

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| Under-cooperation | Contributions are insufficient for the shared goal |
| Fragile cooperation | Cooperation exists but trust or legitimacy is weak |
| High-burden cooperation | Cooperation exists but burdens are unequal |
| Coerced cooperation | Participation is secured through force or fear without legitimacy |
| Symbolic cooperation | Public commitment exceeds substantive contribution |
| Legitimate cooperation | Contribution, trust, legitimacy, fairness, and accountability align |
