# Authority and Legitimacy in Institutions

This advanced companion repository supports the article **Authority and Legitimacy in Institutions** in the Institutional Psychology knowledge series.

The repository provides a research-grade, synthetic-data scaffold for studying how formal authority clarity, procedural legitimacy, outcome legitimacy, trust, rule clarity, social recognition, accountability, repair capacity, fairness, shared norm support, arbitrariness pressure, visible inconsistency, unequal burden, opacity pressure, and enforcement coercion pressure shape authority-legitimacy strength and voluntary compliance.

## Research purpose

This repository is designed for:

- synthetic-data research
- authority-legitimacy modeling
- legitimacy and voluntary-compliance simulation
- procedural-legitimacy diagnostics
- trust and rule-clarity analysis
- accountability and repair-capacity review
- social-recognition modeling
- unequal-burden and opacity-pressure analysis
- arbitrariness and visible-inconsistency diagnostics
- fragile legitimacy-environment assessment
- high-arbitrariness environment review
- legitimacy-repair sensitivity analysis
- public governance, courts, regulators, workplaces, education systems, healthcare systems, platform governance, global governance, and civic institutions examples
- reproducible multi-language workflows
- teaching and public-interest methods demonstration

It is not designed for automated decision-making about real people, employees, citizens, patients, students, applicants, agencies, firms, communities, or institutions.

## Core research question

When does institutional power become accepted authority through fairness, intelligibility, trust, accountability, and repair — and when does authority become brittle, coercive, unevenly recognized, strategically obeyed, or rejected as arbitrary, opaque, exclusionary, or illegitimate?

## Conceptual frame

The scaffold models authority-legitimacy and voluntary compliance as functions of:

- formal authority clarity
- procedural legitimacy

- trust
- rule clarity
- social recognition
- accountability
- repair capacity
- fairness
- shared norm support
- arbitrariness pressure
- visible inconsistency
- unequal burden
- opacity pressure
- enforcement coercion pressure
- fragile legitimacy environments
- high-arbitrariness environments
- legitimacy repair and institutional recognition

## Main variables

| Variable | Meaning |
|---|---|
| formal_authority_clarity | Clarity of offices, roles, decision rights, review pathways, and jurisdiction |
| procedural_legitimacy | Perceived fairness, respect, consistency, transparency, and reviewability of procedure |
| outcome_legitimacy | Perceived adequacy, effectiveness, public value, or tolerability of outcomes |
| trust | Expectations that institutional authority will act consistently and in good faith |
| rule_clarity | Intelligibility of obligations, rights, classifications, and enforcement rules |
| social_recognition | Collective recognition of institutional rightfulness and authority |
| accountability | Credibility of correction, responsibility, appeal, review, and consequences |
| repair_capacity | Capacity to acknowledge, correct, remember, and repair institutional failure |
| fairness | Procedural and distributive fairness across groups and cases |
| shared_norm_support | Social norm supporting compliance with authority or rule |
| arbitrariness_pressure | Inconsistency, opacity, discretionary abuse, or arbitrary treatment pressure |
| visible_inconsistency | Visible gap between stated rule and actual institutional practice |
| unequal_burden | Unequal proof, waiting, surveillance, sanction, or administrative burden |
| opacity_pressure | Difficulty understanding or contesting institutional decisions |
| enforcement_coercion_pressure | Extent to which compliance depends on threat, sanction, surveillance, or force |
| authority_legitimacy_strength | Composite synthetic legitimacy-strength score |
| voluntary_compliance | Composite synthetic voluntary-compliance score |
| high_legitimacy | Binary threshold indicator for high-legitimacy environments |
| high_voluntary_compliance | Binary threshold indicator for high voluntary compliance |
| fragile_legitimacy_environment | High apparent legitimacy with weak procedure and weak trust |
| high_arbitrariness_environment | Strong arbitrariness and visible inconsistency with weak repair capacity |

## Responsible-use note

All data is synthetic. The repository is intended for conceptual modeling, public-interest research, education, and transparent methods demonstration.

Do not use this repository for:

- employee screening
- employment selection
- hiring
- promotion
- compensation
- discipline
- termination
- workplace surveillance
- individual performance management
- psychological assessment
- predictive policing
- automated eligibility determination
- coercive public administration
- authority scoring of real people, groups, communities, or institutions
- legitimacy scoring of real people, groups, communities, or institutions
- ranking real communities, agencies, firms, workers, students, or institutions without context
- demanding obedience from marginalized communities without institutional repair
- treating resistance or distrust as irrational when it may be historically informed
- using legitimacy language to suppress dissent
- replacing rights, appeal, and accountability with appeals to institutional authority
- extracting testimony from affected communities without changing decisions
- legitimacy laundering for already-decided reforms, dashboards, reviews, risk frameworks, governance agendas, or disciplinary regimes

Any real-world adaptation must include legal review, ethical review, qualitative evidence, historical analysis, stakeholder participation, burden audits, affected-community voice, privacy review, and attention to marginalized communities.

## Suggested workflow

1. Read `docs/research-design.md`.
2. Review `ethics/responsible-use-boundaries.md`.
3. Generate synthetic legitimacy data with `python/generate_authority_legitimacy_data.py`.
4. Run dynamic legitimacy simulation with `python/simulate_legitimacy_dynamics.py`.
5. Run fragile legitimacy diagnostics with `python/fragile_legitimacy_environment_review.py`.
6. Run high-arbitrariness environment review with `python/high_arbitrariness_environment_review.py`.
7. Run legitimacy-repair sensitivity analysis with `python/legitimacy_repair_sensitivity.py`.
8. Analyze legitimacy and voluntary compliance in R with `r/authority_legitimacy_modeling.R`.
9. Create SQLite schema with `sql/schema.sql`.
10. Review `validation/validation-plan.md` and `validation/checklist.md`.
11. Use `docs/github-embed-wordpress.html` for the WordPress GitHub block.
