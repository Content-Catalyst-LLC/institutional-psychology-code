# Justice, Historical Memory, and Authority Note

Authority is not legitimate simply because it is formal. It becomes legitimate when it can be defended from the standpoint of those governed, classified, burdened, disciplined, or excluded by institutional power.

A justice-sensitive legitimacy analysis should ask:

- Who recognizes the authority as rightful?
- Who experiences it as coercive?
- Whose distrust is historically grounded?
- Who bears the cost of compliance?
- Who can challenge interpretation, not only outcome?
- Does repair change procedures, incentives, categories, and authority relationships?
- Does the institution preserve memory of harm?
- Is legitimacy language being used to avoid accountability?

Legitimacy should not replace enforceable rights, due process, appeal, transparency, or material repair.
