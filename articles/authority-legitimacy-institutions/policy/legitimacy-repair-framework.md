# Legitimacy Repair Framework

## Legitimacy-repair criteria

A legitimacy-repair system should be evaluated across multiple criteria:

1. **Acknowledgment**  
   Does the institution clearly name the authority failure without euphemism?

2. **Explanation**  
   Does the institution explain what happened, why it happened, and who was affected?

3. **Accountability**  
   Are responsibility and consequences visible and credible?

4. **Correction**  
   Are procedures, incentives, authority structures, data systems, or decisions changed?

5. **Restitution**  
   Are affected people materially helped where possible?

6. **Recognition**  
   Are affected communities treated as legitimate knowers and participants?

7. **Memory**  
   Is harm preserved in institutional memory so recurrence is less likely?

8. **Contestability**  
   Can people challenge not only outcomes, but institutional interpretation?

9. **Verification**  
   Can affected people and external reviewers observe whether repair is real?

## Outcome typology

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| Earned legitimacy | Authority is recognized because institutional conduct makes recognition reasonable |
| Surface compliance | People comply because they must, not because they recognize rightfulness |
| Fragile legitimacy | Apparent legitimacy exists while procedure and trust are weak |
| High-arbitrariness pressure | Arbitrariness and visible inconsistency are high while repair is weak |
| Symbolic legitimacy repair | Messaging or apology appears without material change |
| Justice-centered repair | Affected people help define repair and authority relationships change |
