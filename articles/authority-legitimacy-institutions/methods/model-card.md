# Model Card: Authority and Legitimacy in Institutions Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore authority-legitimacy strength, voluntary compliance, procedural legitimacy, outcome legitimacy, trust, rule clarity, social recognition, accountability, repair capacity, fairness, arbitrariness pressure, visible inconsistency, unequal burden, opacity pressure, and coercion pressure.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers
- organizational researchers
- legitimacy and procedural-justice researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive culture programs
- automated monitoring systems
- surveillance systems
- authority scoring systems
- legitimacy scoring systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing authority and legitimacy conditions.

## Outputs

- authority-legitimacy strength score
- voluntary-compliance score
- high-legitimacy indicator
- high voluntary-compliance indicator
- fragile legitimacy-environment indicator
- high-arbitrariness environment indicator
- legitimacy-repair sensitivity analysis
- institutional authority review tables

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no classification of real actors or communities
- no substitute for legal, historical, qualitative, decision-audit, or institutional expertise

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- legitimacy scoring real communities
- treating resistance as irrational
- replacing rights with authority appeals
- ignoring affected-community knowledge
- legitimizing symbolic repair
- using legitimacy models to manage criticism rather than repair institutions
