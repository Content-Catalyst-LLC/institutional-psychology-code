# Justice, Voice, and Biased Institutional Knowledge Note

Bias is not only a threat to accuracy. It is a threat to justice.

A justice-sensitive bias analysis should ask:

- Whose evidence is treated as objective?
- Whose evidence is treated as anecdotal?
- Whose warning must be repeated before it is believed?
- Who benefits when the institution keeps its old interpretation?
- Who bears the cost of biased judgment?
- Who can challenge official interpretation?
- What evidence is visible in dashboards but invisible in lived experience?
- What lived experience is invisible in dashboards?

Institutions should not use bias language to delegitimize marginalized, frontline, or affected-community knowledge. Bias analysis should make dominant assumptions contestable.
