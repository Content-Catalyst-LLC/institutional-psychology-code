# Method Notes

## Why model cognitive bias institutionally?

Cognitive bias becomes institutionally consequential when it is embedded in procedures, dashboards, categories, committee norms, professional status, reporting architecture, memory, and governance. The purpose of the scaffold is not to diagnose individuals. It is to study the conditions under which institutions transform evidence into judgment, or evidence into confirmation and rationalization.

A useful model can separate:

- overconfidence
- anchoring pressure
- confirmation pressure
- conformity pressure
- filtering distortion
- path lock-in
- metric tunnel vision
- power protection
- dissent capacity
- corrective review
- information quality
- feedback openness
- psychological safety
- justice-sensitive voice

## Why synthetic data?

Synthetic data allows transparent demonstration without using confidential records, vulnerable communities, employees, students, patients, regulated entities, citizens, or sensitive administrative information.

## Why include justice-sensitive voice?

Institutions often treat dominant, technical, managerial, or elite evidence as neutral while treating marginalized or affected-community evidence as anecdotal. Bias analysis must ask whose knowledge is discounted before deliberation begins.

## Why include power protection?

Some biased interpretations persist because correction threatens reputation, authority, legitimacy, budgets, or dominant narratives. Bias is often reinforced by what the institution cannot comfortably afford to know.

## What this scaffold cannot do

It cannot classify real people or institutions as biased. It cannot justify surveillance, coercion, or punitive culture programs. It cannot replace legal analysis, public participation, historical context, qualitative research, or domain expertise.
