# Model Card: Cognitive Bias in Institutional Decision-Making Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore bias pressure, decision quality, overconfidence, anchoring, confirmation pressure, conformity pressure, filtering distortion, path lock-in, metric tunnel vision, power protection, structured dissent, corrective review, information quality, feedback openness, psychological safety, and justice-sensitive voice.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers
- organizational researchers
- decision-quality researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive culture programs
- automated monitoring systems
- surveillance systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing institutional decision-system conditions.

## Outputs

- institutional bias pressure score
- decision quality score
- high resilience decision indicator
- fragile judgment indicator
- high-bias environment indicator
- dynamic bias simulation results
- debiasing sensitivity analysis
- high-bias environment review

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no classification of real actors or communities
- no substitute for legal, historical, qualitative, decision-audit, or institutional expertise

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- labeling dissent as irrational
- treating dominant assumptions as neutral
- ignoring affected-community knowledge
- legitimizing symbolic debiasing systems
- using bias models to manage criticism rather than revise decisions
