# Model Card: Institutional Path Dependence Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore path dependence, lock-in probability, switching costs, and high-burden institutional continuity.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive compliance teams
- automated decision systems
- surveillance systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing institutional path dependence and reform conditions.

## Outputs

- path dependence score
- lock-in probability
- strong lock-in indicator
- high-burden lock-in indicator
- path-strength simulation results
- switching-cost diagnostics
- distributional burden review

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no substitute for historical or qualitative research

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- legitimizing harmful continuity
- ignoring marginalized communities
- treating historical injustice as technical inertia
- using path-dependence models to justify preselected conclusions
