# Method Notes

## Why model enforcement behavior?

Enforcement shapes behavior through monitoring, detection expectations, sanctions, rewards, documentation, corrective action, reputation, trust, and institutional legitimacy. It can produce substantive compliance, but it can also generate fear, concealment, defensive documentation, gaming, selective discipline, and compliance theater.

A useful model can separate:

- monitoring quality
- legitimacy
- incentive alignment
- sanction credibility
- information quality
- adaptive learning
- accountability reach
- compliance burden
- selective enforcement
- evasion pressure
- hypocrisy visibility
- defensive compliance

## Why synthetic data?

Synthetic data allows transparent demonstration without using confidential records, vulnerable communities, employees, students, patients, regulated entities, citizens, or sensitive administrative information.

## Why include fragile enforcement?

An enforcement system can appear strong because it produces audits, sanctions, findings, and reports while still lacking legitimacy, information quality, accountability reach, or learning capacity.

## Why include compliance burden and selective enforcement?

Enforcement distributes scrutiny, cost, documentation, fear, and consequence. Aggregate enforcement may hide unequal burden, unequal appeal capacity, or protection of powerful actors.

## What this scaffold cannot do

It cannot classify real people, firms, agencies, workers, or institutions as compliant, evasive, or legitimate. It cannot substitute for legal analysis, community participation, historical context, qualitative research, or domain expertise.
