# Model Card: Institutional Enforcement and Behavioral Incentives Scaffold

## Model purpose

This scaffold demonstrates how synthetic institutional data can be used to explore enforcement effectiveness, monitoring quality, legitimacy, incentive alignment, sanction credibility, information quality, adaptive learning, accountability reach, compliance burden, selective enforcement, evasion pressure, hypocrisy visibility, and defensive compliance.

## Intended users

- researchers
- students
- policy analysts
- public-interest technologists
- institutional scholars
- governance researchers
- regulatory policy researchers

## Out-of-scope users

- employers evaluating workers
- agencies ranking people or communities
- punitive compliance teams
- automated decision systems
- surveillance systems

## Inputs

Synthetic values representing enforcement and incentive conditions.

## Outputs

- enforcement effectiveness score
- high compliance quality indicator
- fragile enforcement indicator
- high burden enforcement indicator
- dynamic enforcement simulation results
- evasion pressure sensitivity analysis
- compliance burden review

## Limitations

- synthetic data only
- no real causal validation
- no automatic policy recommendation
- no moral or legal responsibility assignment
- no classification of real actors or communities
- no substitute for legal, historical, qualitative, or institutional expertise

## Ethical risks

- false precision
- treating ambiguity or incapacity as evasion
- ignoring affected communities
- legitimizing coercive enforcement
- using enforcement models to discipline visible actors while ignoring power
